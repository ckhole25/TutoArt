BEGINNER LEVEL

Just starting out? Learn the basics of drawing like art elements, simple techniques, beginner tips, and materials you need. Plus, try a face tutorial with a YouTube guide on proportions!

UNDERSTANDING THE BASICS

TYPES OF ELEMENTS IN ART

  • 1. Line: A line is a continuous mark made on a surface. It can vary in width, length, direction, and curvature.
  • 2. Shape: A shape is a self-contained area with height, width, and depth. Shapes can be geometric (like squares and circles) or organic (like free-flowing shapes).
  • 3. Form: Form refers to the three-dimensional quality of an object. It can be created through shading, texture, and other techniques.
  • 4. Value: Value refers to the lightness or darkness of a color or object. It can be used to create contrast, depth, and mood.
  • 5. Texture: Texture refers to the surface quality or "feel" of an object. It can be visual (implied through drawing) or tactile (physical).

BASIC DRAWING TECHNIQUES

  • 1. Hatching: Hatching involves creating a series of parallel lines that follow the contours of an object. It can be used to create texture, shading, and depth.
  • 2. Cross-Hatching: Cross-hatching involves layering hatching lines at different angles to create deeper shading and texture.
  • 3. Stippling: Stippling involves creating an image using small dots. The density and spacing of the dots can be used to create texture, shading, and detail.
  • 4. Gesture Drawing: Gesture drawing involves quickly sketching the overall pose and movement of a subject. It's a great way to capture the essence of a subject.

TIPS FOR BEGINNERS

  • 1. Practice regularly: Regular practice will help you develop your drawing skills and build confidence.
  • 2. Use reference images: Reference images can help you draw more accurately and realistically.
  • 3. Experiment with different materials: Try different drawing tools and materials to find what works best for you.
  • 4. Don't be too hard on yourself: Drawing takes time and practice to develop. Be patient and enjoy the process.

BASIC MATERIALS

  • 1. Pencils: Pencils are a versatile drawing tool that can be used for a wide range of techniques.
  • 2. Erasers: Erasers are used to correct mistakes and create highlights.
  • 3. Paper: Choose a paper that is suitable for drawing, such as sketch paper or cartridge paper.
  • 4. Sharpener: A sharpener is used to keep your pencils sharp and ready for use.

BASIC FACE TUTORIAL

Step-by-Step Guide to Basic Face Proportions

  • Step 1: Draw a Head Shape
    Start with an oval or circle for the general head shape. If you want a more structured approach, lightly sketch a vertical line down the center to help with symmetry.
  • Step 2: Divide the Face into Halves
    Draw a horizontal line across the middle of the face—this will be the guideline for the eyes. Divide the lower half into two sections—this helps place the nose and mouth.
  • Step 3: Place the Eyes
    The eyes generally sit along the middle horizontal guideline. The space between the eyes should be roughly one eye-width.
  • Step 4: Add the Nose
    The bottom of the nose usually aligns with the first lower division of the face. Its width should be roughly as wide as the space between the inner corners of the eyes.
  • Step 5: Position the Mouth
    The mouth sits at the second lower division of the face. The corners of the mouth usually align with the middle of each eye.
  • Step 6: Add the Ears
    Ears start at the eye level and end at the bottom of the nose.
  • Step 7: Draw the Hairline
    The hairline is placed roughly one-third of the way down from the top of the head.
